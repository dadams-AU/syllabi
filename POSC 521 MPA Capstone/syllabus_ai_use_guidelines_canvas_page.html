Syllabus AI Use Guidelines

Responsible use of generative AI in reading, writing, and analysis

Generative AI tools may be used as learning tools within clear boundaries. They can help students test ideas, receive critique, and reflect on their work. They may not replace reading, analysis, or authorship. All submitted writing must remain the student's own work.

Policy in one sentence: AI may be used for critique, questions, and basic mechanics checks when permitted, but not for drafting, rewriting, summarizing, or polishing submitted work.

What Is Allowed?

  • Using AI to critique your work by identifying strengths, gaps, counterarguments, missing concepts, or weak connections.
  • Using AI to generate questions for discussion or reflection.
  • Using AI feedback to plan future improvement when an assignment allows a feedback appendix.
  • Using tools such as Grammarly for spelling, grammar, punctuation, and formatting checks only.
  • Using approved alternatives to AI, such as peer feedback or a structured self-critique, when permitted.

What Is Not Allowed?

  • Using AI to outline, summarize, draft, rewrite, paraphrase, or polish submitted prose.
  • Pasting AI-generated sentences or paragraphs into an assignment as if they were your own writing.
  • Using AI to do the reading or substitute for your own analysis and judgment.
  • Using grammar tools or writing assistants to transform style, structure, or argument.
  • Treating AI output as authoritative without checking it for accuracy, fairness, and relevance.

Assignment-Specific Boundaries

Rough Drafts

Initial or rough drafts for major writing assignments should be written entirely by the student with no AI assistance. That includes no AI outlining, summarizing, drafting, rewriting, or editing.

Diagnostic Feedback

When an assignment includes a Feedback Appendix, students may use AI to request critique. In these cases, AI is functioning as a reviewer, not a co-author.

Diagnostic Only Rule

If an assignment labels the appendix Diagnostic Only, the AI feedback may not be used to revise that same submission. It is intended to improve future work rather than change the current graded draft.

Required Documentation

  • When AI feedback is permitted and used, include it in a Feedback Appendix.
  • List the tool name and date used.
  • Briefly indicate how you evaluated the feedback or what you plan to do with it.
  • Do not treat AI output as a source to quote or build into your prose unless the assignment explicitly allows that use.

Quick Reference

Do Do Not
Use AI to critique clarity, evidence, organization, and counterarguments. Use AI to draft, rewrite, or polish submitted text.
Use AI to generate discussion or reflection questions. Use AI to summarize readings instead of doing the reading.
Include permitted AI feedback in the feedback appendix. Use diagnostic-only feedback to revise the same-week submission.
Use grammar tools for mechanics checks only. Use grammar tools to paraphrase or transform style and structure.
Keep your own voice and reasoning central. Treat AI output as correct without evaluation.

Why These Boundaries Exist

  • They preserve authorship and academic integrity.
  • They keep reading, interpretation, and argument development in the student's hands.
  • They help students use AI as a feedback partner rather than a substitute thinker.
  • They build professional judgment about when AI is useful and when it distorts learning.
  • They support ethical use of tools that may reflect bias, hallucinate facts, or mishandle sensitive information.

Ethics and Responsible Use

  • Authorship: All submitted prose must be written by the student.
  • Attribution: When AI feedback is used, document the tool and date in the feedback appendix.
  • Bias Awareness: Evaluate AI outputs critically for bias, omission, and distortion.
  • Verification: Confirm any facts, citations, or claims before using them.
  • Confidentiality: Do not upload sensitive, private, or proprietary information into AI tools.
  • Sustainability: Use AI thoughtfully and avoid unnecessary or excessive prompting.

Possible Consequences of Misuse

Misuse may include submitting AI-generated writing as your own, failing to disclose permitted AI use, or relying on AI in ways that violate assignment rules. Consequences may include a required revision, grade penalties, or referral through academic integrity procedures.

Suggested Student Reminder

Your voice, reading, and reasoning must remain central. If you are unsure whether a specific AI use is allowed, ask before submitting the assignment.
